Malay Cuisines

Food Menu

  • Nasi Lemak

    A fragrant rice dish cooked in coconut milk and pandan leaf.

  • Satay

    Grilled meat skewers served with peanut sauce.

  • Nasi Kerabu

    Blue-colored rice is eaten with dried fish or fried chicken, crackers, pickles and other salads..

  • Chicken Rendang

    Dry chicken curry made from coconut milk, spices, and aromatics like garlic, ginger, and lemongrass.

  • Lemang

    The rice is cooked in coconut milk and placed in bamboo sticks along with banana leaves just to keep separate the rice from bamboo sticks.

Drink Menu

  • Teh Tarik

    A cup of "pulled tea" made of strong, locally-sourced black tea combined with either condensed or evaporated milk .

  • Sirap Bandung

    A drink that consists of evaporated milk or condensed milk flavoured with rose syrup, giving it a pink colour.

  • Air Jagung

    A drink which key ingredients include tender corn kernels, milk, sugar, and occasionally coconut milk or condensed milk.

  • Milo Dinosaur

    A drink that composed of a cup of iced Milo with undissolved Milo powder added on top of it. .

Dessert Menu

  • Pisang Goreng

    A fritter made by deep frying battered banana or plantain in hot cooking oil.

  • Cendol

    An iced sweet dessert that contains droplets of pandan-flavoured green rice flour jelly, coconut milk and palm sugar syrup.

  • Otak-otak

    A Southeast Asian fish cake made from minced fish mixed with spices, wrapped in a leaf wrapper, and served steamed or grilled.

  • Kuih Seri Muka

    A two-layered dessert with steamed glutinous rice forming the bottom half and a green custard layer made with pandan juice.

  • Bubur Cha Cha

    A dessert and breakfast dish that prepared using pearled sago, sweet potatoes, yams, bananas, coconut milk, pandan leaves, sugar and salt.
