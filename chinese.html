Chicken Rice

Chicken Rice is a simple yet beloved dish in Malaysian Chinese cuisine. It consists of poached or roasted chicken served over fragrant rice cooked in chicken fat and broth. The dish is typically accompanied by a dipping sauce made from garlic, and chili, soy sauce as well as a side of soup

Cantonese Claypot Chicken Rice

Claypot Chicken Rice is cooked in a claypot with marinated chicken, Chinese sausages, and sometimes salted fish. The claypot gives the rice a crispy bottom and infuses the dish with a smoky flavor. The chicken is tender, and the dish is garnished with spring onions and soy sauce.

Fried Rice

Fried Rice is a versatile dish where rice is stir-fried with eggs, vegetables, and meat like chicken or prawns. It's seasoned with soy sauce and garlic, making it a quick, flavorful comfort food. The dish is often customized with different ingredients, reflecting local tastes and creative personal preferences.

Sticky Rice Dumplings

Zongzi, or Sticky Rice Dumplings, are made for the Dragon Boat Festival. These dumplings, filled with glutinous rice, marinated pork, and salted egg yolk, are wrapped in bamboo leaves and boiled until tender, resulting in a savory, aromatic treat. The dumplings are especially popular during the Dragon Boat Festival.

Noodles

Hokkien Mee

Hokkien Mee is a well-known Southeast Asian cuisine, especially in Malaysia and Singapore, with variations across the region. It is a stir-fried noodles, typically composed of yellow egg noodles. The dish often includes ingredients like prawns, and chicken, sometimes pork belly or fish cake.

Char Kway Teow

Char Kway Teow is a popular stir-fried noodle dish made from flat rice noodles, typically stir-fried with eggs, prawns, Chinese sausages (lap cheong), bean sprouts, and chives in a mix of dark soy sauce and chili paste. It is seasoned with a hint of smoky flavor from the wok, making it a rich and flavorful dish.

Curry Laksa

Curry Laksa is a spicy noodle soup made with coconut milk, curry, and various ingredients such as tofu puffs, fish cakes, shrimp, and bean sprouts. The noodles can be rice vermicelli or yellow egg noodles or even mix of the two. This dish is known for its creamy and aromatic broth, which is both spicy and savory.

Assam Laksa

Assam Laksa is a tangy and spicy fish-based noodle soup. The broth is made from tamarind (assam), which gives it a sour flavor, and is cooked with mackerel, lemongrass, and other herbs. It's usually served with thick rice noodles and garnished with cucumber, pineapple, mint, and shrimp paste (hae ko).

Pastry

Mooncake

Mooncake is a traditional pastry enjoyed during the Mid-Autumn Festival. It has a thin crust and is filled with sweet red bean or lotus seed paste, often with a salted egg yolk center, symbolizing the full moon.

Rice Ball

Tangyuan, or Rice Balls, are glutinous rice balls usually filled with sweet fillings like sesame paste, red bean paste, or peanut butter. They are often served in a sweet ginger syrup or soup and are traditionally eaten during the Lantern Festival.

Kaya Toast with soft-boiled eggs

Kaya Toast is a popular breakfast item, featuring toasted bread spread with kaya, a sweet coconut jam, and butter. It's typically paired with soft-boiled eggs, seasoned with soy sauce and white pepper.

Beh Teh Soh / Heong Peng

Beh Teh Soh, also known as Heong Peng, is a traditional Chinese pastry filled with a sticky, sweet maltose filling. The pastry is flaky and baked until golden brown, offering a delightful contrast between the crisp exterior and the gooey center.
