Recommended Malaysian Chinese Cuisine Restaurant

Rice Dishes

Nasi Ayam Hainan Chee Meng

Malaysian, Chinese

1. 50, Jalan Bukit Bintang, Kuala Lumpur
2. Sub Lot A-1, Batu 4 1/4, Jalan Kelang Lama, Kuala Lumpur
3. 20, Jalan U1/36, Hicom Glenmarie Industrial Park, Shah Alam, Selangor

The restaurant is well-known among locals for its signature Hainanese chicken rice. At Nasi Ayam Chee Meng, they take immense pride in their offerings, especially the food. With fantastic service, a cozy atmosphere, and authentic Hainanese cuisine, they aim to make every dining experience a memorable one.

Hong Kee Claypot Chicken Rice

Malaysian, Chinese


83, Jalan Sultan, City Centre, 50000 Kuala Lumpur, Wilayah
Persekutuan Kuala Lumpur

At Hong Kee, the claypots of chicken rice are cooked over a traditional charcoal fire. The small claypot comes with tender chicken, slices of Chinese sausage, salted fish, and is topped with fragrant oil, dark soy sauce, and an optional raw egg. The egg not only adds a rich flavor but also coats the rice, making it smooth without the need for excessive oil.

Noodle dishes

Penang Ah Leng Char Koay Teow

Malaysian, Chinese

343, Jalan Dato Keramat, George Town, 10150 George Town, Pulau Pinang, Malaysia

The kuay teow was light and perfectly separated, with a satisfying hint of wok hei. You can choose a regular plate of char kuay teow for RM6, or the special version with mantis prawns for RM10.

Damansara Uptown Hokkien Mee

Malaysian, Chinese

117, 119, 121, Jalan SS 21/37, Damansara Utama

Serving traditional-style Hokkien Mee in a newly renovated, air-conditioned setting, this restaurant ensures a satisfying dose of wok hei in every bite. Be sure to try the roast duck, fried chicken, and chili lala—these crowd favorites are highly recommended! The restaurant also has two additional branches located in Puchong and Kota Damansara.

Pastry

Sin Guan Hoe Biscuit

Malaysian, Chinese

23, Jalan Pasir Bendamar, 36000Teluk Intan, Perak

Heong Peng from Kedai Biskut Sin Guan Hoe in Teluk Intan is a well-known local specialty. This version is distinguished by its rich and flavorful filling, complemented by a generous amount of sesame sauce that enhances its taste and aroma. The Heong Peng is priced at RM 6.50.

Ching Han Guan Biscuit

Malaysian, Chinese

145, Jalan Sultan Iskandar 30000 Ipoh, Perak

This famous Ipoh biscuit shop is renowned for its traditional pastries, including floss biscuits and mooncakes. They use high-quality ingredients and craft their biscuits with care and passion. Their handmade biscuits are always fresh, offering unique textures and a less sweet taste, thanks to the use of natural flavors.
